ground storage
Path: CARNOT/storage

Purpose
Three-dimensional ground storage model.

Description
This is a three dimensional model of a thermal ground storage where the heat is transferred in the ground by a pipe heat exchanger in an S-form. The pipe is lying in the depth z_pipe in a rectangular area (similar to an earth collector for a heat pump). The heat transport in the ground is calculated with a finite difference scheme (Patankar, 1980).

      rho*cp * dT/dt = div (cond * grad T) + S

where S is the source term. For nodes with the heat exchanger pipe

      S = U * A / dV * (Tfluid - T)

The plate is divided in two horizontal layers and three nodes in the direction of the pipes. Perpendicular to the pipes, the plate has one node for each pipe and one node between the pipe-nodes. The minimum is three nodes in this direction. The ground is divided relative to the number of nodes in the horizontal direction. There are two nodes on each side outside the plate:

  • the first is the same size as the plate nodes
  • the next is 50% larger, extending away from the plate.

Vertically, the top three nodes are equal to half the height of the pipe depth (= 0.5*z_pipe). For each consecutive node the height is increased by 0.5*z_pipe. The fourth node has a height of z_pipe, the fifth node of 1.5*z_pipe, ... You may specify a refining factor to increase the number of nodes in each direction. A factor of two gives the double number of nodes in each direction, in total 8 times the initial number of nodes. According to Albers (1991) the thermal properties of the ground can be classified into only four types. The filling of the area above the heat exchanger can be concrete.

Inputs:

  • Weather data vector
  • temperature above heat exchanger area [°C]
  • THVin
  • temperature at ground surface [°C]
  • temperature below storage [°C]

Outputs:

  • THVout
  • temperatures at measurement points

The measurement points are below the outlet of the pipe. The sensors are distributed over the height (first sensor is at top, last at bottom). The surface models of the subset "surface model for ground storage" are used in the subsystem of the ground storage. The Weather data vector is transformed to a heat transfer vector for the region outside of the heat exchanger area. Specify the albedo of the ground for this region. For the region above the heat exchanger an isolated cover model is used. Specify the heat transfer coefficient for this region. You may use the open ground model or the isolated cover model for both regions. Just remove the old blocks and copy a new one at this place. (Break the library link before, otherwise you will modify the CARNOT-library.)

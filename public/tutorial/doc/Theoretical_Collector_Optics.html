collector_optics
Path: CARNOT/heat_source/incidence angle modifier (IAM)

Purpose
The block calculates the absorbed radiation from the optical characteristics of the type of glass used for the collector cover.

Description
This block transforms the incoming direct and diffuse radiation per unit area to absorbed solar power. It uses the optical efficiency of the collector, direct and diffuse radiation on the aperture area and the incidence angles. There are four different types of collector glazing in the Library:

  • double glazing extra white glass
  • single glazing extra white glass
  • double glazing float glass
  • single glazing float glass

The incidence angle modifier (IAM) is derived from Fresnels equations for glass material with a refraction index 1.526, an extinction coefficient of 4/m (extra white glass) or 16/m (float glass) and a thickness of 4 mm. See "taualfa" in the manual or type "help taualfa" on the command line for more information on the calculation of the IAM.

Input:

  • radiation (vector with column 4:7 of the Weather data vector)
  • tetap, tetas (incidence angles in the longitudinal and transversal collector plane)

Output:

  • absorbed radiation per surface area in [W/m2] (the block uses the optical efficiency "eta0")

See "taualfa" in the manual or type "help taualfa" on the command line for more information on the calculation of the IAM.

NOTE: When fitting the measured values with "taualfa", you should vary the thickness of the cover and the extinction coefficient. For some collectors you will get unusual values for the parameters. Due to shading and other physical effects, the IAM of measured collectors is usually lower than the physical properties of the cover, calculated by the function "taualfa".

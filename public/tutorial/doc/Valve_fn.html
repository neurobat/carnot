valve
Path: CARNOT/hydraulics

Purpose
The block calculates the pressure drop at a valve. Keep a small pressure drop even if valve is completely open.

Description
The valve increases its pressure drop according to the position. One means completely open, zero means closed. A specific valve characteristic is used to compute the resistance coefficient f in:

dp = f (Reynolds) * r/2 * v2

f increases at low Reynolds numbers. A maximum ceta of 100 is set with a valve position close to 0. Minimum f is 1 at position = 1. Below valve position 0.01 the valve sets the flow_ID to a negative value. The following flow mixer will interpret the branch to be closed.

You may adapt the valve characteristics to your demand. See the look-up table "valve characteristic" in the subsystem valve/pressuredrop/c*r2*v2

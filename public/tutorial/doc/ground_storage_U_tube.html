ground_storage_U-tube
Path: CARNOT/storage

Purpose
Model for the calculation of an U-tube ground storage.

ATTENTION: The c-souce code of the model is not completely validated.

 

Description
This block contains the model of an U-tube ground storage the heat is transferred in the ground by a U-tube heat exchanger in the bore. The surrounding surface is modelled for two different cases that can be found in the subset surface models for the ground storage. The heat transport in the ground is calculated with a two-dimensional finite difference scheme using the cylindrical symmetry (Patankar, 1980).

      rho*c * dT/dt = 1/r * d/dr (r * cond * dT/dr) + d2T/dz2+ S

where S is the source term. For the central node (bore)

      S = (UA + cond * 4 / dborehole) / dV * (Tfluid - T).

The storage is divided into ZNODES nodes in z-direction, RNODES nodes in radial direction and 6 nodes in angular direction. The energy balance for every node is:

       rho*c*dT/dt = cond/dz2 * (Tnode_up+Tnode_down - 2*Tnode) + cond*(r+dr/2)/(r*dr2) * (Tnode_out - Tnode) + cond*(r-dr/2)/(r*dr2) * (Tnode_in - Tnode)

According to Albers (1991) the thermal properties of the ground can be characterized by only four types. For the bore, a fifth type of filling can be specified (betting bore filling) (Reuss, 1993).
An insulation material between descending and rising pipe is not calculated. Research shows that the performance of the U-tube decreases when using the insulation (Reuss, 1993). This is due to the reduction in the heat transfer surface of the pipe: about one third of the perimeter is covered by the insulation.

In axial direction the nodes have the same extension (same height), in radial direction the size increases with the radius. The size of the node is proportional to its distance from the centre.

This is better to model the fast changes near the heat exchanger pipe. The boundary conditions for the outer nodes are specified at the surface (top node) and at the bottom. For the nodes in-between the temperature is linearly interpolated.

Inputs:

  • Weather data vector
  • THVin
  • temperature at ground surface [°C]
  • temperature below storage [°C]

Outputs:

  • THV
  • temperatures at measurement points

Because you need to know the temperature at fixed places inside the storage, a number of measurement points (M_PTS) is placed at equidistant locations inside the storage, no matter how many nodes are used for the calculation. The output vector starts with the top-temperature and ends with the bottom-temperature (M_PTS).
Three measurement points are arranged in a layer:

  • first at the centre of the storage
  • second at half of the radius
  • third at the outer boundary.

The layers are distributed over the height range (first sensor is at top, last at bottom). One of the surface models in the subset "surface models" is used in the subsystem of the ground storage. The Weather data vector is transformed to a heat transfer vector for the surface. Specify the albedo of the ground for this region. You may use the open ground model or the insulated cover model for both regions. Just remove the old blocks and copy a new one at its place (break the library link of the storage before doing that, otherwise you will change the CARNOT library).
Validation:
This model has not yet been thoroughly tested. There is only few measured data available for this type of storage. The model has been compared with the data published by Reuss (Reuss 1988), mainly with the data obtained in the laboratory model of a U-tube ground storage.
Attention:The model does not include water transport phenomena. Specially for higher temperatures (> 40°C) the water transport has high impact on the heat transport and the model might not be correct any longer.

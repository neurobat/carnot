Purpose
Collector model, that calculates the THV output vector.

Description
The collector model is a one-dimensional multinode model for thermal collectors. The model is based on parameters obtained from measurement, i.e. collector-test results. The collector is divided in "Node" nodes. The flow is equally distributed among the risers. The energy balance for every node is a differential equation, that is calculated in the S-function unicol.

ccoll * dT/dt = qsolar + mdot*c/A * (Tin - T)

      + U1*(Tamb - T) - U2*(Tamb - T)2

      + Uwind*vwind*(Tamb - T) + Usky*(Tsky - T)

The collector calculates friction losses in the massflow. This may be specified in the subsystem "pressure drop" of the collector (see also subsystem "pressure drop of collector" in carnot/heat_source. Static pressure is calculated if necessary, i.e. for thermosyphon systems.
See chapter "basics concepts, THV-Vector".

Inputs:

  • Weather data vector
  • collector position
  • Thermo-Hydraulic Vector collector inlet

Outputs:

  • Thermo-Hydraulic Vector collector outlet
  • collector surface Acoll [m2]
  • optical efficiency
  • heat loss coefficient u1[W/(m2K)]
  • quadratic heat loss coefficient u2[W/(mK)2]
  • radiative heat loss coefficient usky[W/(m2K)]
  • wind dependent heat loss coefficient uwind[W/(m/s*m2*K)]
  • length between inlet and outlet
  • heat capacity of the collector ccoll[J/(m2K)]
  • initial temperature Tini [°C]
  • number of nodes

Validation

  • The model was checked with results of earlier TRNSYS simulations of the Solar Campus Jülich, where the Isakson collector model (type 101) was used. The discrepancy in power was smaller than 2 %.
  • A comparison of the model with test data of a flat plate collector has been done and shows low difference (validation report).

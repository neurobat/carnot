pump
Path: CARNOT/Pumps

Purpose
Pump model which uses user defined pump characteristics.

Description
You may specify your own pump specifications by entering the three coefficients a0, a1, a2 of a quadratic polynomial, that approximates the characteristics of the pump.
With the coefficients the pump sets the massflow as a result of the pressures. The pressure drop in the system is given by [con lin qua] in the THV:
The two pressures must be the same. The resulting equation is solved analytically in the pump. The direct feedthrough in the temperature is broken by a transfer function with a time-constant of 100 s. To get the electrical power consumption, you have to specify a pump efficiency characteristic in a look-up table. The calculation is carried out in an S-function.

NOTE:If you use a control-signal less than 1, the pump is still working. But the pump characteristic does not represent the real pump any more!

NOTE:If you use free components of the THV, you have to change the Pumps. To avoid algebraic loops, unused components of the THV are not fed through.

Parameters and Dialog Box

  • diameter at in and outlet [m]
  • fluid [water,air,cotton oil, silicone oil, water glycol]
  • percentage of fluid mixture [0..1]
  • pump constant coefficient [Pa] (must be > 0)
  • pump constant coefficient [Pa] (usually < 0)
  • pump constant coefficient [Pa] (must be < 0)
  • system pressure [Pa]
  • level of pressure calculation [only friction, friction and thermal head]
